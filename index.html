Proxima Centauri b:

Proxima Centauri b is an exoplanet that orbits Proxima Centauri, the closest star to our Solar System. It is about 4.24 light-years away from Earth, making it the nearest known exoplanet to us. The planet is slightly larger than Earth, with a mass of at least 1.17 times Earth's. It orbits its star every 11.2 days because it is much closer to its star than Earth is to the Sun. Even though it is so close to its star, Proxima Centauri is a cool red dwarf, so the planet is in the habitable zone where liquid water could exist. Scientists think Proxima Centauri b is a rocky planet, but they are not sure whether it has an atmosphere or oceans.

Proxima Centauri b was discovered in 2016 by an international team of astronomers led by Guillem Anglada-Escudé. They found the planet using the radial velocity method, which measures tiny movements in a star caused by the gravity of an orbiting planet. The discovery was made using the HARPS spectrograph at the European Southern Observatory in Chile after months of careful observations.

Scientists believe Proxima Centauri b is one of the best places to search for life because it is close to Earth and lies in the habitable zone. If the planet has an atmosphere and liquid water, it could possibly support simple forms of life. However, Proxima Centauri is a very active star that produces powerful solar flares and radiation, which could damage or even strip away the planet's atmosphere. Because of this, scientists do not know if life could survive there. More research and future space missions will help us learn whether Proxima Centauri b is truly habitable.
